# Renaissance Art

The Renaissance was a period in European history marking the transition from the Middle Ages to modernity, covering the 15th and 16th centuries. Renaissance art emerged in Italy in the late 14th century and reached its zenith in the early 16th century.

## Characteristics

### Humanism
Renaissance art reflected the intellectual movement of humanism, which emphasized the value and potential of human beings. Artists depicted religious subjects with a new focus on human emotion, anatomical accuracy, and individual personality.

### Perspective
The development of linear perspective was one of the defining technical innovations of Renaissance art. Filippo Brunelleschi is credited with formulating the mathematical principles of perspective around 1415. This allowed artists to create the illusion of three-dimensional space on a two-dimensional surface.

### Naturalism
Renaissance artists strove for naturalistic representation, studying anatomy through dissection, observing light and shadow (chiaroscuro), and depicting the natural world with unprecedented realism.

## Major Artists

### Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519)
The quintessential "Renaissance man," Leonardo excelled as a painter, sculptor, architect, scientist, and inventor. His masterpieces include the "Mona Lisa" and "The Last Supper." Leonardo's sfumato technique — the subtle blending of colors and tones — created an atmospheric softness that became highly influential.

### Michelangelo (1475-1564)
Michelangelo was a sculptor, painter, and architect whose work embodied the High Renaissance ideal. His marble statue of "David" and the ceiling frescoes of the Sistine Chapel in Vatican City are among the most recognized artworks in history.

### Raphael (1483-1520)
Raphael was celebrated for the grace and clarity of his compositions. His "School of Athens" fresco in the Vatican's Apostolic Palace is a masterwork of perspective and a visual summation of Renaissance humanism, depicting philosophers and scientists from classical antiquity.

## Regional Schools

### Italian Renaissance
Centered in Florence, Rome, and Venice, the Italian Renaissance was characterized by its emphasis on classical antiquity, mathematical perspective, and the patronage of wealthy families like the Medici.

### Northern Renaissance
In Flanders, Germany, and the Netherlands, artists like Jan van Eyck and Albrecht Dürer developed a distinctive style characterized by meticulous detail, rich symbolism, and the perfection of oil painting techniques.
