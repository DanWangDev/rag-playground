# World War II

World War II or the Second World War was a global conflict that lasted from 1939 to 1945. The vast majority of the world's countries, including all of the great powers, fought as part of two opposing military alliances: the Allies and the Axis.

## Causes

### Treaty of Versailles
The harsh terms imposed on Germany after World War I, including massive reparations payments and territorial losses, created deep resentment and economic instability that extremist political movements exploited.

### Rise of Fascism
The 1920s and 1930s saw the rise of fascist regimes in Germany under Adolf Hitler and in Italy under Benito Mussolini. These regimes promoted aggressive nationalism, militarism, and territorial expansion.

### Appeasement
Western democracies, particularly Britain and France, initially pursued a policy of appeasement toward German territorial demands in the hope of avoiding another major war. The Munich Agreement of 1938, which allowed Germany to annex parts of Czechoslovakia, is the most notable example.

## Major Events

### Invasion of Poland (1939)
Germany invaded Poland on September 1, 1939, using blitzkrieg ("lightning war") tactics. Britain and France declared war on Germany two days later, marking the official start of World War II.

### Pearl Harbor (1941)
Japan's surprise attack on the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, on December 7, 1941, brought the United States into the war. The attack destroyed or damaged nearly 20 American naval vessels and over 300 airplanes.

### D-Day (1944)
The Allied invasion of Normandy, France, on June 6, 1944, was the largest seaborne invasion in history. It established a Western front against Germany and marked the beginning of the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi control.

### Atomic Bombings (1945)
The United States dropped atomic bombs on the Japanese cities of Hiroshima (August 6) and Nagasaki (August 9), 1945. These remain the only uses of nuclear weapons in armed conflict. Japan surrendered on August 15, 1945.

## Aftermath

The war resulted in an estimated 70-85 million fatalities, the majority of whom were civilians. The Holocaust, Nazi Germany's systematic genocide of approximately six million Jews, stands as one of history's greatest atrocities. The United Nations was established in 1945 to foster international cooperation and prevent future conflicts.
